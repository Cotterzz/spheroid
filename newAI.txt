We need to rethink the AI, it is based on very simple, kinda fudged foundations. Further development has improved it slightly but not enough.
We should redraw the original plan but with more detail:

A. Gamestates (or whatever nomenclature is appropriate)

1. I have the ball
2. teammate has the ball
3. Same but I'm closest
4. Opponents have the ball
5. No-one has the ball, I am closest.
6. No-one has the ball, I am second closest.
7. Goal scored, get into starting position.

B. Player roles

1. Attack, R L
2. Defend, R L
3. Goalkeeper

C. Targets.

This is a great abstraction, targets *can* teleport, but the players just turn round with new heading, which is smooth.
We also need firing targets, for passing and shooting, and logic to determine which/when/if.

Possibly even multiple move targets that give player options, taking the nearest?

the way we organise states with movement targets should be a big part of this.

eg, 1. i have the ball:

1. I want to move into empty space, away from other players on both sides, preferably up the field, but if I see a good shooting or passing opportunity (player is in better place than me, or i need to send it back in retreat/defense) I should take it. Also move backward if absolutely necessary to avoid opponents.

It gets complex because we need a set of priorities for all states and all roles. Then we need to make sure the mechanics work too.
